Crop Residue to Elecrticity

Within fifty kilometres of the plant, there would be a number of collecting centres, making it simple for farmers without access to transportation to supply their agricultural residues. Farmers will be compensated after delivery. The leftovers will then be brought to the factory, which houses the storage facilities. To maintain a steady supply for the plant to run on, there will always be a sizable store of crop waste. The paddy straw-based biomass power plants will convert crop residue into electricity.

Recycling Crop Residue

Traditional farming practises have resulted in decreased soil organic matter, increased soil erosion, and contaminated surface and groundwater. Soil quality in agricultural lands benefits greatly from effective crop residue management. Crop residues can be used in the field in a variety of ways, either by being left in the open field or being included in other field operations like ploughing. By adding organic manure to the soil, this improves its ability to store water, reduces weed growth and soil erosion, and increases the activity of the soil's microorganisms. Crop residues increase the organic matter in the soil, which enhances the soil's structure and quality.

Soil Aeration

By encouraging a free interchange of gases between the soil and the atmosphere, crop waste cover enhances soil aeration. This is made easier by boosting soil drainage, porosity, and structural stability, further reducing surface crusting.

Weed Control

Crop wastes operate as a mulch in the soil, which inhibits the growth of weeds. This layer limits the amount of sunlight and nitrogen that can reach the soil, which also prevents weeds from growing nearby.
